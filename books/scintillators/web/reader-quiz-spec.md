## Purpose

The book's Preface defines three reading paths. The quiz turns that prose into an interactive front door:

1. Helps the reader land on the right entry point (so they actually read the book).
2. Captures name + email so Berkeley Nucleonics can build a reader list.
3. Offers an opt-in for a free **printed hard copy**.
4. Offers an opt-in for a **class pass to academy.berkeleynucleonics.com**.

It is a value-first lead magnet — readers get something useful (a personalized reading roadmap) and BNC gets a list. No login. No paywall. No dark patterns.

---

## Three reader paths (from the Preface)

| ID | Persona | The book's prescribed path |
|---|---|---|
| `student` | Graduate student or new hire | Read straight through. Ch 1–9 build foundation, Ch 10+ gets practical. Use Glossary (App G) and skip "Going Deeper" sidebars on first pass. |
| `buyer` | Technical buyer / applications engineer specifying a detector | Start with **Ch 8 (selection)**, use **Appendix B** cross-reference matrix to find your application, jump back into **Ch 3 (materials)** and **Ch 11 (configurations)**. **Appendix D** for regulatory. |
| `designer` | Designer building or specifying a custom detector | **Ch 9 (photodetectors) → Ch 11 (configurations) → Ch 13 (electronics)**. **Appendix A** for what's shipping today and in 18 months. |

**Scoring:** Each option awards points to one or more of `student`, `buyer`, `designer`. Highest total wins. Ties resolve in this priority order: `designer > buyer > student` (because design intent is the most specific signal).

**No back button needed at this length, but provide one for forgiveness.**

## Result pages

Each result page contains:
1. **The path label** ("You're on Path 1: Foundation Reader" etc.).
2. **One-sentence why** — why this path was chosen, in the book's voice.
3. **The reading roadmap** — a numbered list of chapters in suggested order, with a single line on each.
4. **The lead-capture form** (always shown).
5. **Two opt-ins** — printed hard copy + class pass to academy.berkeleynucleonics.com.

### Path 1 — Foundation Reader (`student`)

**Headline:** *You're on the foundation track — read straight through.*

**Why:** You said you want to learn the field, you have time, or some of the terminology is still new. The book was structured for this. Don't skip the early chapters; everything later builds on them.

**Roadmap:**
1. **Ch 1 — Why scintillation, why now.** Five-page orientation to the field.
2. **Ch 2 — Scintillation physics.** From photon to count.
3. **Ch 3 — Properties and use of scintillation materials.** The reference table you'll come back to.
4. **Ch 4 — Thermal neutron detection.**
5. **Ch 5 — Radiation damage.**
6. **Ch 6 — Emission spectra and photodetector matching.**
7. **Ch 7 — Temperature influence.**
8. **Ch 8 — Choosing the scintillator for your application.** This chapter is the spine of the book.
9. **Ch 9 — Scintillation light detection devices.**
10. From here, **Ch 10–14 get practical** — detector configurations, electronics, the Nuclear Renaissance.
11. **Appendix G — Glossary** is your friend. Keep it open in another tab.
12. **Skip the "Going Deeper" sidebars on a first pass** — come back to them when you need the math.

**Encouragement line:** *The book is meant to be marked up. Read it with a pen.*

---

### Path 2 — Application Selector (`buyer`)

**Headline:** *You're on the selection track — start with Chapter 8.*

**Why:** You have an application in mind and need to specify the right detector. The book gives you a five-question decision tree, then a use-case matrix, then enough materials and configuration detail to defend the choice.

**Roadmap:**
1. **Ch 8 — Choosing the scintillator for your application.** Start here. The five questions, in order, plus the decision tree and worked examples.
2. **Appendix B — Use Cases & Applications Catalog.** Find your application in section B.{n}. Use the cross-reference matrix in B.10.
3. **Ch 3 — Properties and use of scintillation materials.** The reference for the materials Ch 8 sends you to.
4. **Ch 11 — Detector configurations.** How those materials become a deliverable detector.
5. **Appendix D — Standards, Regulations & Certifications.** What the regulators will require.
6. **Appendix A — New Materials Deep Dive.** What's shipping in 18 months, in case your spec horizon is more than a year out.
7. **Optional: Ch 14 — Detectors for the Nuclear Renaissance** if you're touching SMR, fusion, or microreactor work.

**Encouragement line:** *Chapter 8 is the most consequential decision in this book. Spend an hour there before doing anything else.*

---

### Path 3 — Custom Designer (`designer`)

**Headline:** *You're on the design track — start with Chapter 9.*

**Why:** You're building or specifying hardware. You don't need a tutorial on what NaI is. You need photodetector choices, configuration mechanics, and the electronics chain.

**Roadmap:**
1. **Ch 9 — Scintillation light detection devices.** PMTs, SiPMs, dSiPMs, MCP-PMTs.
2. **Ch 11 — Detector configurations.** Mechanical, optical, hermeticity, coupling.
3. **Ch 13 — Detector electronics.** Front-end, pulse processing, timestamped readout.
4. **Appendix A — New Materials Deep Dive.** What's on the shelf today and what's shipping in 18 months. Spec for tomorrow, not yesterday.
5. **Ch 6 — Emission spectra and photodetector matching.** Cross-check your scintillator-photodetector pairing.
6. **Ch 5 — Radiation damage** if your environment exposes the detector to a hard field.
7. **Ch 14 — Detectors for the Nuclear Renaissance.** New configurations being asked for that didn't exist as catalog items five years ago.
8. **Appendix F — MCA & Reference Guide.** For the readout side.

**Encouragement line:** *The readout side moved faster than the materials side. If you haven't designed with modern SiPMs yet, Chapter 9 will reset what you think a detector chain looks like.*

## How the form delivers data

The HTML quiz POSTs to a public form-to-email endpoint (Formspree, Web3Forms, or Basin) configured to email `davidbrown750@gmail.com` (or a BNC alias). David swaps the endpoint URL into one constant at the top of the file. No backend code required.

The full quiz answers, the computed path, and all opt-ins are included in the email payload so David has complete context for fulfillment.

See `scintillator-quiz-README.md` for setup steps.
